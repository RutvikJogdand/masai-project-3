Engineering is one of the most popular field to go for in our country(India). There are some people who do well in it no doubt and have charted out a good career path for themselves. But,

  • A huge chunk of the 15 lakh engineers who graduate earn 3-4 lakhs per annum or even less than that.
  • A majority are stuck doing monotonous and mundane jobs which don't contribute positively to their personal growth, knowledge or career.
  • A lot of these then go for MBA or higher education after being frustrated with what they are doing and hope that things will change after they get that next degree.
  • On a personal note, I've seen a lot of these engineers from different branches(Mechanical engineering, Electronics, Telecommunications..etc) work in an IT company. Very few land up in their desired profile. I've seen a lot of them earning meager salaries and be disappointed with the work they are doing.
I went into engineering expecting to be taught things that would help me build cool things and work on technologies through which i could build a cool app or tech. It did not go the way i expected. A lot of focus in the college was on passing exams and maintaining a CGPA above 7 so that in the end you could get a nice job. A lot of engineering colleges these days are run like a business where the focus is on getting students placed rather than getting them educated and really learn and solve problems through thinking.
